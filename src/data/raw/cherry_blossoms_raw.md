The cherry blossom (æ¡œ, sakura) is Japan's unofficial national [flower](https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2423.html). It has been celebrated for many centuries and holds a prominent position in Japanese culture. There are dozens of cherry tree [varieties](https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2011_species.html) in Japan, most of which bloom [for just a few days in spring](https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2011_when.html). The seasonal spectacle is celebrated with [hanami](https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2011_how.html) (cherry blossom viewing) parties under the trees.
